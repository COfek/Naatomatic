# How the duty fairness works (Justice Table), in plain language

Duty is shared fairly, but **Keva (career)** and **Sadir (mandatory)** are handled
differently.

**Keva — fixed annual quota.** Each Keva member targets **2 week-long + 4
single-day** shifts per calendar year. The load is spread **evenly among Keva**
(whoever has done the fewest goes next). If you do more or fewer than your fair
share in a year, the difference **carries to next year** (do extra now → owe fewer
next year, and vice-versa). A year-straddling shift counts in the year it **starts**.

**Sadir — balanced by "burden points."** No fixed cap; instead everyone's load is
tracked and the **least-loaded eligible person goes next**. Points are kept in
**two separate pools** that don't trade off:
- **Shifts pool** — week-long (7), single-day (1), and ad-hoc (0.5 × days).
- **Support pool** — the 24/7 customer-support standby (1 per weekday, 2 for a Fri–Sat weekend).

So doing lots of support never excuses you from guard shifts, and vice-versa. The
pools are cumulative, so if you cover for someone now you're picked less next time
(and they're picked more) — that's the built-in compensation.

**Who's eligible at all** is checked first: right population/rank, the duty-type
flag set, range-qualified for guard duty, not on an approved date-block, and not
already on another assignment at the same time. Among everyone eligible, fairness
(above) decides who's actually picked.

Ask the assistant to *"explain why I was/wasn't picked"* or *"show the justice
standings"* and it will walk through these for your case.
