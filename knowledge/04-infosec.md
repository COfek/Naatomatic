# Information security — what NOT to do in the office

> **DRAFT placeholder — replace with the branch's real security policy.** The items below are sensible defaults, not the official policy.

The branch runs **separate networks by classification** (Civilian, Global, Secret,
Top-Secret). Keeping them separate is the whole point — mixing them is a serious
security incident.

**Do not:**
- **Connect a computer to a port of the wrong classification**, or re-patch a wall jack, **without permission**. A Secret machine on a Global port (or vice-versa) is a cross-domain breach. Network changes go through a **request → network-manager resolution**, never ad-hoc.
- **Move or swap equipment between people yourself.** Equipment is signed out and returned through Logistics so custody is always tracked.
- **Plug in personal/unauthorized devices** (USB drives, phones) to classified machines.
- **Share your access** or let someone act under your identity.

**Do:**
- Use only the port/classification you're authorized for.
- Open a ticket for any network or equipment change.
- Report anything that looks wrong (a mis-patched jack, an unexpected device) to the network manager.

When in doubt, **ask before you connect**. The assistant validates every action
against the rules and will refuse an unsafe one — but the first line of defense is
not attempting it.
